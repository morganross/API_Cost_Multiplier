Quick update (as of October 12, 2025)

- Status — shutdown now in effect. Federal funding lapsed at 12:01 a.m. EDT on October 1, 2025 after the Senate failed to advance stopgap bills (the House “clean” CR failed in the Senate). ([pbs.org](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/amp/politics/partial-government-shutdown-begins-as-nation-faces-new-period-of-uncertainty?utm_source=openai))

- Scale — large workforce & cost. The nonpartisan CBO estimated roughly 750,000 federal employees could be furloughed each day of a lapse (about $400 million in wages withheld per day); agencies are executing contingency plans. ([washingtonpost.com](https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/2025/10/01/government-shutdown-affects-agencies-closed-open//?utm_source=openai))

- Why it happened — political impasse over policy: Democrats have demanded extensions/changes to Affordable Care Act premium tax credits and related health funding; House Republicans pushed a “clean” short-term CR (to Nov. 21) that could not overcome the 60‑vote threshold in the Senate. ([pbs.org](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/amp/politics/partial-government-shutdown-begins-as-nation-faces-new-period-of-uncertainty?utm_source=openai))

- Recent actions and disruptions — administration moves and agency effects:
  - President Trump directed the Defense Department to use available funds so military personnel will be paid on Oct. 15. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/8601ea092fe746cc35b88076e70c4598?utm_source=openai))  
  - The OMB has signaled and begun reduction‑in‑force steps; Reuters and other outlets report layoff notices and agency cuts (including reported firings at CDC and staff reductions at multiple departments). ([reuters.com](https://www.reuters.com/world/us/us-government-workforce-cuts-have-begun-omb-chief-says-2025-10-10/?utm_source=openai))

- What’s being affected now — essential entitlement programs (Social Security, Medicare) continue, but many discretionary functions are paused or reduced (national parks, some research and inspection activities, passport processing, parts of USDA/FDA/NIH/CDC operations); FAA/TSA staffing strains may affect travel. Expect more service slowdowns the longer the shutdown lasts. ([washingtonpost.com](https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/2025/10/01/government-shutdown-affects-agencies-closed-open//?utm_source=openai))

- What to watch next — possible paths to reopen: (1) a Senate compromise that wins Democratic votes (e.g., by adding health‑care subsidy language), (2) targeted short-term funding for specific agencies, or (3) prolonged stalemate with mounting economic and political pressure. Key near-term dates: Oct. 15 (military paycheck) and scheduled court actions over layoffs. ([pbs.org](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/amp/politics/partial-government-shutdown-begins-as-nation-faces-new-period-of-uncertainty?utm_source=openai))
